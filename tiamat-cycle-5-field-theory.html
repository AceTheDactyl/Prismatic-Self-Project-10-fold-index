8.1 Continuum Embedding

The discrete oscillator phases (theta_1, ..., theta_N) are embedded into a continuous complex-valued order parameter field defined on the spatial domain Omega contained in R^3:

Definition 8.1 (Complex Order Parameter Field)

The continuum field psi(x, t) in C is constructed as a superposition of localized contributions from each oscillator:

psi(x, t) = Sigma_i alpha_i(t) * G_sigma(x - x_i) * e^{i*theta_i(t)}

where the sum runs over all N = 5 oscillators, each located at position x_i in Omega.

8.1.2 Gaussian Kernel

Definition 8.2 (Gaussian Smearing Kernel)

The normalized Gaussian kernel in d = 3 dimensions:

G_sigma(x) = (2*pi*sigma^2)^{-3/2} * exp(-|x|^2 / 2*sigma^2)

This kernel satisfies the normalization condition:

integral_R^3 G_sigma(x) d^3x = 1
Convolution Property

The Gaussian kernel is the unique function that is both rotationally invariant and separable in Cartesian coordinates. It also satisfies G_sigma1 * G_sigma2 = G_{sqrt(sigma1^2 + sigma2^2)}, making it natural for coarse-graining procedures.

8.2 Coherence Length from L_4

Theorem 8.1 (Kernel Width Identification)

The kernel width sigma is uniquely determined by the L_4 gap constant:

sigma = Delta = phi^{-4} = 0.1458980337503154... where phi = (1 + sqrt(5))/2 is the golden ratio

8.2.1 Physical Interpretation

The coherence length sigma = Delta has three equivalent interpretations:

Spatial Coherence

The characteristic distance over which the phase of psi remains correlated. At distance r = sigma, the influence of an oscillator decays to 1/e of its peak value.

Resolution Scale

The minimum spatial scale at which the continuum description is valid. Features smaller than sigma are averaged out by the Gaussian smearing.

Healing Length

The scale over which the field "heals" from a local perturbation. If psi is distorted at a point, it returns to equilibrium over a distance of order sigma.

8.3 Ginzburg-Landau Functional

The field dynamics derive from a free energy functional that encodes the energetic cost of spatial gradients, local deviations from equilibrium, and inter-oscillator coupling.

Theorem 8.2 (Free Energy Functional)

The Ginzburg-Landau free energy is:

F[psi] = integral_Omega d^3x [ (1/2)|nabla psi|^2 + V(|psi|^2) + (1/2) Sigma_{ij} K_ij(x)|psi_i - psi_j|^2 ]

8.3.1 Gradient Term

The kinetic energy density (1/2)|nabla psi|^2 penalizes spatial variations in the field:

(1/2)|nabla psi|^2 = (1/2)(nabla psi^*) * (nabla psi) = (1/2)[|nabla|psi||^2 + |psi|^2|nabla arg(psi)|^2]
Phase-Amplitude Decomposition

Writing psi = rho * e^{i*phi}, the gradient term splits into:
Amplitude gradient: (1/2)|nabla rho|^2 penalizes density variations
Phase gradient: (1/2)*rho^2|nabla phi|^2 penalizes phase twisting (superfluid velocity)

8.3.4 Coupling Term

The final term encodes the Kuramoto-type coupling between oscillators:

(1/2) Sigma_{ij} K_ij(x)|psi_i - psi_j|^2

where K_ij(x) is the spatially-varying coupling strength between oscillators i and j. This term is minimized when psi_i = psi_j (phase synchronization).

8.4 Time-Dependent Ginzburg-Landau Equation

The dynamics of the field psi(x, t) follow from gradient descent on the free energy functional, ensuring that F[psi] decreases monotonically in time.

Theorem 8.4 (TDGL Equation)

The time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau equation is:

partial psi / partial t = -delta F / delta psi^*

8.4.1 Full Derivation

Functional Derivative Computation:
  1. Gradient term: delta/delta psi^* [(1/2)|nabla psi|^2] = -(1/2)*nabla^2 psi
  2. Potential term: delta/delta psi^* [V(|psi|^2)] = (dV/drho)*psi = [-(K/2) + (Delta/2)|psi|^2]*psi
  3. Coupling term: delta/delta psi^* [(1/2) Sigma_j K_ij|psi - psi_j|^2] = Sigma_j K_ij(psi - psi_j)
  4. Combine: -delta F/delta psi^* = nabla^2 psi + K*psi - Delta|psi|^2*psi - Sigma_j K_ij(psi - psi_j)
partial psi / partial t = nabla^2 psi + K*psi - Delta|psi|^2*psi - Sigma_j K_ij(psi - psi_j) Time-Dependent Ginzburg-Landau (TDGL) equation

8.4.3 Gradient Descent Interpretation

Variational Principle

The TDGL equation is a gradient flow on the infinite-dimensional manifold of field configurations. At each instant:

dF/dt = integral_Omega |partial psi / partial t|^2 d^3x ≤ 0

The free energy decreases at a rate equal to the squared L^2-norm of the velocity field. This guarantees convergence to local minima (stationary configurations).

8.4.4 Connection to Kuramoto Dynamics

Lemma 8.1 (Kuramoto Reduction)

In the limit of uniform amplitude |psi| = const and point-like oscillators, the TDGL equation reduces to the Kuramoto model:

Reduction Procedure:
  1. Write psi = |psi|*e^{i*theta} with |psi| = sqrt(K/Delta) = const
  2. Substitute into TDGL, project onto imaginary part
  3. The amplitude equation is automatically satisfied
  4. Phase equation: partial theta / partial t = -Im[e^{-i*theta}*Sigma_j K_ij(e^{i*theta} - e^{i*theta_j})]
  5. Simplify: d theta_i / dt = Sigma_j K_ij * sin(theta_j - theta_i)
d theta_i / dt = omega_i + Sigma_j K_ij * sin(theta_j - theta_i) Kuramoto equation recovered (with natural frequencies omega_i from heterogeneity)
Universality

The TDGL framework thus unifies:
- Kuramoto oscillators (phase dynamics at fixed amplitude)
- Pattern formation (amplitude dynamics at fixed phase)
- Full coupled dynamics (amplitude-phase interplay)

The Kuramoto model is the "phase-only" sector of the full Ginzburg-Landau theory.

8.5 Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking

The potential V(rho) possesses a continuous U(1) symmetry that is spontaneously broken by the ground state configuration.

Definition 8.4 (U(1) Symmetry)

The free energy F[psi] is invariant under global phase rotations:

psi(x, t) -> e^{i*alpha} * psi(x, t) for all alpha in [0, 2*pi)
Theorem 8.5 (Goldstone Mode)

Spontaneous breaking of U(1) implies the existence of a massless mode (Goldstone boson) corresponding to uniform phase rotations of the condensate.

The synchronized oscillator state is mathematically a superfluid of phase coherence.

8.6.3 Why the Synchronized State Has n = 0

Topological Triviality of Synchronization

The synchronized state psi = |psi|_min * e^{i*Psi(t)} with spatially uniform phase Psi has:

  • Vanishing phase gradient: nabla arg(psi) = nabla Psi = 0
  • Zero winding number: n = (1/2*pi) oint nabla Psi . dl = 0 for any contour
  • Minimum energy: No gradient energy contribution from phase

Vortices (n != 0) represent topological excitations above the ground state, with energy cost E ~ K*ln(L/sigma). They are suppressed in the synchronized regime where K is large.

9. Renormalization Group Analysis

The renormalization group (RG) provides a systematic framework for understanding how the effective parameters (K, Delta) change under coarse-graining, revealing the fixed points that govern the macroscopic behavior.

9.1 Coarse-Graining Procedure

Definition 9.1 (Block-Averaged Field)

At scale l, define the coarse-grained field:

psi_l(x) = integral d^3y W_l(x - y) psi(y) where W_l is a Gaussian window of width l
RG Transformation Steps:
  1. Integrate: Sum over field modes with wavevector |k| in [Lambda/b, Lambda]
  2. Rescale momenta: k -> b*k so that cutoff returns to Lambda
  3. Rescale fields: psi -> b^{d_psi}*psi to maintain canonical normalization
  4. Read off: New effective couplings K'(K, Delta), Delta'(K, Delta)

9.2 Beta Functions

Theorem 9.1 (RG Flow Equations)

Under infinitesimal rescaling x -> x/b = x*e^{-dl}, the couplings flow according to:

dK/dl = (2 - d)*K + beta_K(K, Delta)
dDelta/dl = (4 - d)*Delta + beta_Delta(K, Delta)

In d = 3 dimensions, with one-loop corrections:

beta_K = -K + c_K * K^2/Delta c_K = 1/(8*pi^2) at one-loop
beta_Delta = Delta + c_Delta * Delta^2 c_Delta = 3/(16*pi^2) at one-loop

9.3 Fixed Points

Definition 9.2 (Fixed Point)

A fixed point (K*, Delta*) satisfies dK/dl = dDelta/dl = 0, corresponding to scale-invariant physics.

9.5 Correlation Functions

The two-point correlation function G(x - y) = angle-bracket psi(x) psi^*(y) angle-bracket encodes the spatial structure of fluctuations and is the primary observable connecting theory to experiment.

9.5.3 Ordered Regime (Exponential Decay)

Theorem 9.4 (Ordered Phase Correlations)

Deep in the synchronized phase (K >> K_c), fluctuations about the condensate decay exponentially:

G(r) ~ exp(-r / xi) Exponential decay with correlation length xi

The correlation length diverges as the critical point is approached:

xi = sigma * |K - K_c|^{-nu} with correlation length exponent nu = 1/2 (mean-field) or nu = 0.67 (3D Ising)
