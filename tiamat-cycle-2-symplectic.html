3.1 Symplectic Form

Definition 3.1 (Canonical Symplectic Form)

The cotangent bundle T*T⁵ carries the canonical symplectic form:

ω = Σᵢ dpᵢ ∧ dθᵢ(3.1)

This is exact: ω = dλ where λ = Σᵢ pᵢ dθᵢ is the Liouville 1-form.

Symplectic Structure on T*T⁵

    Properties:
    • ω is closed: dω = 0
    • ω is non-degenerate: ω⁵ ≠ 0

3.2 Reduced Phase Space

The system has a U(1) symmetry under global phase rotation θᵢ → θᵢ + α. The corresponding momentum map is:

J = Σᵢ pᵢ Total phase momentum (conserved under U(1) action)

Symplectic reduction at J = 0 yields the reduced phase space:

P_red = J⁻¹(0)/U(1) ≅ T⁴ × ℝ⁴(3.2)
Key Insight

The reduced phase space has dimension 8 = 2(N-1) = 2(5-1). One degree of freedom is removed by fixing the total momentum, and one by quotienting out the U(1) action. The dynamics on P_red capture the relative phases between oscillators.

3.5 Liouville's Theorem and Volume Preservation

Theorem 3.2 (Liouville's Theorem)

The Hamiltonian flow preserves the symplectic volume form:

d/dt (ω⁵) = 0 Phase space volume is conserved along trajectories

This implies that attractors cannot exist in the full Hamiltonian formulation. However, the Kuramoto model has implicit dissipation through the frequency distribution, allowing synchronization as an effective attractor in the reduced dynamics.
