3. Black Hole Thermodynamics

3.1 Schwarzschild Geometry

For a non-rotating black hole of mass M, the event horizon radius is:

R_s = 2GM / c²
Schwarzschild Radius

The event horizon area:

A_H = 4πR_s² = 16πG²M² / c⁴
Horizon Area

3.2 Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy

The entropy of a black hole is proportional to its horizon area, not volume. This is the holographic principle in action:

S_BH = (k_B c³ A_H) / (4Gℏ) = (k_B A_H) / (4ℓ_P²)
Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy
Key Insight: Entropy scales as M², meaning it scales with area (not volume). A 1 solar mass black hole encodes ~10⁷⁷ bits on its horizon—more than all particles in the observable universe!

3.3 Hawking Temperature

Black holes emit thermal radiation at temperature:

T_H = (ℏc³) / (8πGMk_B)
Hawking Temperature

Note: Smaller black holes are hotter (T ∝ 1/M).

3.4 First Law of Black Hole Thermodynamics

dM = T_H × dS_BH
First Law (analogous to dU = TdS)
