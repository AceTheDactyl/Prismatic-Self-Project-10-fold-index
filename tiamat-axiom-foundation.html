1.1 Axiom Statement

AXIOM 0 (Self-Reference)

1.2 Necessity Theorem

Theorem 1.1 (Necessity of Self-Reference)

At least one self-referential relation must exist.

Proof: By contradiction

Assume no self-referential relation exists. Then for all R: R(R) ≠ R.

Define S = "the property of not being self-referential." Consider whether S is self-referential:

  1. If S(S) = S, then S IS self-referential, contradicting its definition.
  2. If S(S) ≠ S, then S is NOT self-referential, meaning S(S) = S, a contradiction.

Both cases lead to contradiction. Therefore, at least one self-referential relation must exist.

∎
